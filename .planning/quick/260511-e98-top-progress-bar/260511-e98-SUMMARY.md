# Quick Task e98: Top Progress Bar Summary

**One-liner:** Slim rust progress bar fixed to top of slide viewport, width-driven by `useNav().currentPage / total`, mounted via Slidev's `global-top` auto-injected global layer.

## What Was Built

A single Vue 3 SFC at `/global-top.vue` (project root) that Slidev auto-discovers and renders above every slide on every layout. The component:

- Imports `useNav` from `@slidev/client` and computes `progress = (currentPage / total) * 100`.
- Renders a 3px-tall fixed bar at `top: 0` with a transparent track and an inner fill colored `var(--accent)` (rust `#CC785C`).
- Animates width changes with a 300ms ease-out transition.
- Uses sharp corners (D-05) and z-index 100 to sit above slide content.
- Marked `aria-hidden="true"` and `pointer-events: none` — purely decorative.

  gsd-progress-top__fill[data-v-1dff3ac2]{background-color:var(--accent);will-change:width;height:100%;transition:width .3s ease-out}

## Slidev Convention Used

`global-top.vue` (kebab-case) placed at the **project root** is one of three Slidev "global layers" files (`global-top.vue`, `global-bottom.vue`, `custom-nav-controls.vue`). Per [sli.dev/features/global-layers.html](https://sli.dev/features/global-layers.html), Slidev auto-detects these files and integrates them into the presentation. The `global-top` layer renders above slide content but is excluded from presenter chrome / sidebar thumbnails — satisfying D-14/D-15 scoping rules without explicit `.slidev-layout` guarding.
